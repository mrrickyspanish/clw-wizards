# New Family Onboarding Interview

Use this guide with Tyler to document the real process a new parent should follow before the public New Families page is treated as final operational guidance.

## 1. Initial Interest

- What should a completely new parent do first?
- Should they email, call, message Facebook, visit a practice, or create an account?
- Who owns new-family inquiries?
- How quickly should a family expect a response?
- Is there a preferred contact method?

## 2. Visiting the Club

- Can a parent and child visit before joining?
- Is there a free trial practice, observation day, or introductory session?
- Does the wrestler participate during the first visit or only observe?
- Does the visit need to be scheduled in advance?
- Is a waiver required before a child steps onto the mat?
- Which practice or group should a first-time family attend?
- Can parents remain in the room during the visit?
- Who greets and speaks with the family when they arrive?

## 3. Formal Registration

- At what point should the parent create an online account?
- Does creating an account officially register the wrestler?
- Does a club leader need to approve the registration?
- Can families join after the season has started?
- Is there a registration deadline?
- Is there a capacity limit or waiting list?
- What confirms that registration is fully complete?

## 4. Practice Group Placement

- Does the club currently use three groups or four groups?
- What are the official group names?
- Who selects the wrestler's initial group?
- Should parents select a group during onboarding, or should staff assign it?
- What factors determine placement: age, weight, experience, maturity, technique, or coach evaluation?
- Can wrestlers move groups during the season?
- How and when is a placement change communicated?

## 5. Costs and Requirements

- What is due when a family first joins?
- What must be completed before the first practice?
- What are the current club dues or registration costs?
- What is the current raffle or fundraising requirement?
- What does a USA Wrestling athlete membership currently cost?
- Is the singlet deposit still required, and what is the current amount?
- What tournament costs should families expect?
- Which costs are optional?
- Are scholarships, payment arrangements, or hardship support available?
- Are refunds available if a wrestler decides not to continue?

## 6. Equipment and First Practice

- What should a wrestler wear for a first visit or trial?
- Are wrestling shoes required immediately?
- Is headgear required immediately?
- Does the club have loaner equipment?
- What should parents bring?
- How early should a new family arrive?
- What forms, waivers, or identification are needed?
- What should the family expect during the first practice?

## 7. Parent Expectations

- Which groups allow parents to watch practice?
- Are there different rules during the first two weeks?
- How are behavior, attendance, and pickup expectations communicated?
- Are parents expected to volunteer, help at tournaments, or support fundraising?
- Is a coaching card required for any parent who enters the wrestling room?

## 8. Communication After Joining

- How does the family receive the practice schedule?
- Where are cancellations and urgent updates posted?
- What communication happens through the parent portal?
- What communication happens through email, text, or Facebook?
- Who should parents contact with coaching, billing, uniform, or tournament questions?

## 9. Parent Portal Workflow

- What should the parent portal handle immediately after account creation?
- Which athlete fields are truly required during onboarding?
- Should practice group be removed from parent selection and assigned by staff?
- When are dues created or assigned?
- Which documents must be uploaded?
- How are tournament registrations approved and paid?
- What should the dashboard show a brand-new family before a wrestler is formally placed?

## 10. Website Copy to Confirm

Confirm or replace the current public guidance for:

- Whether families should contact the club before visiting
- Whether a trial or observation option exists
- Whether beginners are welcome throughout the season
- The official age range
- Official practice group names and descriptions
- Current required and optional costs
- Equipment needed for the first visit versus regular practice
- Parent access to the practice room
- The exact meaning of “Create Parent Account”
- The point at which a wrestler is considered officially enrolled

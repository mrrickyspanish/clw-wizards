# Creative Output Rules

Version: 2.0

## Purpose

These rules guide creative work for RJ across image prompts, visual concepts, logos, overlays, thumbnails, posters, brand systems, social graphics, and design critiques.

## Core principle

Creative work must serve the use case.

Do not create something visually impressive but strategically wrong.

## High-impact image prompt standard

For high-impact image creation requests, use structured prompts with these sections:

- Subject
- Environment
- Composition
- Lighting
- Energy or Motion Suggestion
- Texture and Detail Level
- Color Strategy
- Rendering Style

Before creating the final prompt, internally clarify:

- The core moment of the image
- The emotional tone
- The brand implication
- What must be avoided
- Camera angle choice
- Lighting rationale
- Color strategy
- Composition intent and power dynamic

Do not expose internal analysis unless RJ asks.

## Avoid generic AI aesthetics

Avoid:

- Random glow effects
- Over-saturated cyberpunk looks
- Generic neon lighting
- Stock-photo vibes
- Unfocused visual clutter
- Plastic skin
- Fake text
- Overly glossy fantasy render style
- Excessive lens flare
- Unnecessary background elements
- Multiple unrelated concepts when one execution was requested

## Respect the ask

If RJ asks for a 16:9 desktop image, deliver 16:9.

If RJ asks for no copy or graphics, do not add copy or graphics.

If RJ asks for an overlay, do not make a thumbnail.

If RJ asks for a professional sports logo, do not make 3D fantasy art.

If RJ asks for a white background, do not leave unwanted black areas.

If RJ asks to update an existing prompt with new feedback, update that prompt. Do not create a separate unrelated brief.

## Logos and brand marks

Logo work should feel professional, usable, and system-minded.

Avoid:

- Fantasy illustration unless requested
- 3D mascot render style unless requested
- Overcomplicated detail
- Too many effects
- Poor scalability
- Fake typography
- Unusable lockups
- Random symbols

Prefer:

- Strong silhouette
- Clear hierarchy
- Scalability
- Simple marks
- Sports-logo discipline when relevant
- Brand system thinking
- Clear color versions
- Transparent-background exports when requested

## Website visual direction

Website visuals should support:

- Trust
- Clarity
- Conversion
- Brand tone
- Readability
- Hierarchy
- Mobile execution

Avoid:

- Decorative clutter
- Unnecessary animations
- Random gradients
- Tiny body copy
- Generic SaaS blocks
- Overbuilt sections
- Designs that force extra scrolling without benefit

## YouTube and video overlays

Overlays should fit the video use case.

For vertical footage inside landscape frames:

- Preserve the main video slot.
- Make empty space intentional.
- Use brand elements lightly.
- Leave room for topic labels only when requested.
- Avoid destination-specific visuals unless the video is destination-specific.
- Make the overlay reusable for tips, rankings, on-ship content, listicles, reviews, and personality-driven videos.

## Posters and stylized images

When RJ asks for a movie-poster style, capture the energy and layout logic without turning it into random parody clutter.

Focus on:

- Composition
- Subject hierarchy
- Cinematic mood
- Genre cues
- Typography space if copy is requested
- Strong focal point

## Product and shop creative

Product creative should support conversion.

Focus on:

- Product clarity
- Brand fit
- Lifestyle context
- Mobile readability
- Clean hierarchy
- Trust
- What the buyer gets

Avoid making products feel more complicated than they are.

## Creative critique standard

When critiquing visuals, use:

- What works
- What weakens it
- Why it matters
- What to change
- Updated prompt or instruction if useful

Be specific.

Do not say "make it pop."

## Output discipline

For creative deliverables:

- Give one strong execution when RJ asks for one.
- Give variations only when RJ asks for exploration or when strategic options are genuinely useful.
- Label what each version is for.
- Do not overwhelm with choices.
- Prioritize the strongest path.

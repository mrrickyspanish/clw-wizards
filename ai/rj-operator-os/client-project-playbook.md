# Client Project Playbook

Version: 2.0

This playbook applies to RJ's client-facing work across websites, content, branding, proposals, video, social media, and digital marketing systems.

## Core positioning

RJ helps businesses look more credible, communicate more clearly, and build digital systems that support real business activity.

Most client work should connect deliverables to business outcomes.

Do not present work as "just a website," "just posts," or "just graphics" when the real value is clarity, trust, and execution.

## Common client project types

RJ's client work may include:

- Website design
- Website development
- Homepage refinement
- Service page copy
- SEO passes
- Copy and conversion audits
- Landing pages
- Social media content
- Video editing briefs

- Launch campaigns

## Client discovery standard

Before creating a plan, clarify or infer:

- Who the client serves
- What the business offers
- What action the audience should take
- What makes the client credible
- What is confusing now
- What proof exists
- What assets are available
- What needs to be built
- What should not be overbuilt

## Website project standard

For websites, evaluate:

- Homepage clarity
- Offer clarity
- Audience fit
- Section flow
- CTA path
- Contact path
- Mobile readability
- Service page specificity
- Trust cues
- SEO basics
- Visual hierarchy
- Technical risk
- Launch readiness

Do not redesign the site unless the mission calls for it.

## Homepage refinement standard

A homepage should answer:

1. What is this business?
2. Who is it for?
3. What problem does it solve?
4. Why should I trust it?
5. What should I do next?

Improve:

- Hero headline
- Intro copy
- CTA language
- Service clarity
- Proof
- Section flow
- Mobile readability
- Conversion path

Avoid:

- Generic hero language
- Overlong explanations
- Decorative clutter
- Adding sections without purpose
- Copy that sounds like every other agency or small business site

## Service page standard

A service page should explain:

- Who the service is for
- What is included
- What business problem it solves
- What outcome it supports
- Why the client is credible
- What the next step is

Do not just list deliverables. Tie deliverables to business value.

## Work or portfolio page standard

A work page should not just show visuals.

When possible, clarify:

- The client context
- The problem
- The solution
- The business value
- The deliverables
- The result or intended effect
- Related services

Do not invent metrics.

## About page standard

An about page should build trust.

It should explain:

- Who is behind the business
- Why the business exists
- Why the audience should trust them
- How the work is approached
- What kind of client or customer they serve

Avoid making it too corporate, too biography-heavy, or too generic.

## Contact page standard

A contact page should reduce hesitation.

It should clarify:

- How to reach out
- What happens after someone reaches out
- What information helps start the conversation
- Expected next step, if known
- Low-friction CTA

Avoid making contact feel like a big commitment unless that is intentional.

## SEO pass standard

For client pages, review:

- Page title
- Meta description
- H1
- H2s
- Natural service keywords
- Local relevance when appropriate
- Internal links
- Alt text
- Thin content
- Duplicate metadata

Do not keyword-stuff.

## Proposal standard

A proposal should include:

- Client context
- Recommended solution
- Business reason
- Scope
- Deliverables
- Investment
- Timeline assumptions
- Responsibilities
- Next step
- Optional add-ons only when useful

Do not add services just to make the proposal bigger.

## Social content standard

A client content system should define:

- Audience
- Brand voice
- Content pillars
- Recurring formats
- Capture needs
- Posting rhythm
- Approval process
- Sample posts
- Success indicators

Keep it practical for the client's actual capacity.

## Video brief standard

A video brief should define:

- Audience
- Desired feeling
- Story arc
- Pacing
- B-roll
- Graphics
- Music
- Platform format
- Deliverables
- Review criteria

Do not make the editor guess the goal.

## Launch QA standard

Before launch, verify:

- Pages load
- Mobile layout
- Desktop layout
- CTAs
- Forms
- Links
- Metadata
- Images
- Basic accessibility
- No placeholder text
- No broken nav
- No unrelated production risks

## Client communication standard

Client-facing language should be:

- Clear
- Professional
- Warm
- Specific
- Scope-aware
- Value-focused

Avoid:

- Overpromising
- Jargon
- Fake urgency
- Agency clichés
- Long explanations that bury the point

# Conversion Standards

Version: 2.0

## Purpose

These standards define how agents should improve conversion for RJ's websites, client pages, proposals, landing pages, shops, and contact flows.

Conversion means helping the right person take the next useful step.

It does not mean making everything louder.

## Core conversion questions

Every important page should answer:

1. Am I in the right place?
2. Is this for someone like me?
3. What problem does this solve?
4. Why should I trust this?
5. What happens if I take the next step?
6. What should I do now?

## CTA standard

CTAs should be clear, natural, and appropriate to the visitor's stage.

Good CTA examples:

- Start a Project
- Let's Talk
- Talk Through Your Project
- See What We Can Build
- Get Started
- Request a Quote
- Plan Your Trip
- Book a Consultation
- Contact Us

Avoid:

- Fake urgency
- Too many CTAs
- Vague "Learn More" everywhere
- Aggressive sales language
- CTAs that overpromise

## Trust cue standard

Trust can come from:

- Specific services
- Clear process
- Real work examples
- Client context
- Before and after explanation
- Testimonials
- Credentials
- Experience
- Honest next steps
- Visual polish
- Clear contact path

Do not invent proof.

## Page flow standard

A page should move logically:

1. Problem or need
2. Offer or solution
3. Why it matters
4. Proof or detail
5. Process or what to expect
6. CTA

Not every page needs every step, but the flow should feel intentional.

## Service conversion standard

A service page should convert by making the service easier to understand and trust.

It should explain:

- Who the service is for
- What is included
- What problem it solves
- What outcome it supports
- Why the provider is credible
- What the next step is

## Contact conversion standard

A contact page should lower friction.

Clarify:

- Why someone should reach out
- What information helps
- What happens next
- Whether the first step is low pressure
- How to contact

Avoid making the visitor feel like they need everything figured out before reaching out.

## Shop conversion standard

A shop page should prioritize:

- Product clarity
- Product images
- Price and purchase path
- Trust
- Mobile usability
- Reduced distraction
- Clear benefits
- Clear shipping or expectation details where applicable

Avoid clever copy that hides what the item is.

## Proposal conversion standard

A proposal should convert by making the recommendation feel obvious.

It should include:

- Client problem
- Recommended solution
- Deliverables
- Business value
- Investment
- Timeline assumptions
- Next step

Avoid overloading the proposal with optional extras unless they clearly help.

## Conversion audit output

Use this format:

### Desired Action

State the action.

### Biggest Friction

Identify the largest conversion problem.

### Fixes

List fixes by impact.

### Copy or CTA Updates

Show changed language if relevant.

### Remaining Risk

Call out what still needs proof, data, or RJ taste.
